# Taste Principles

These are durable quality rules learned from real corrections.

## Current principles

1. Prefer explicit structure over vague strategic prose.
2. Prefer checklists and review loops over abstract intentions.
3. Prefer a canonical workspace over scattered notes.
4. Prefer markdown-first systems before adding databases or automation.
5. Prefer phased implementation over all-at-once redesigns.
6. Capture corrections as reusable assets instead of leaving them in chat history.
7. Challenge false binaries instead of accepting oversimplified either/or framing.
8. Assume nonlinearity in strategic problems and surface interacting variables.
9. Reframe the problem at least once before committing to a neat answer.
10. Prefer desirable difficulty now over delayed discomfort later.
11. For high-risk questions, prefer a concise answer with only the critical caveats over either reckless certainty or sprawling hedging.

## Promotion rule

A principle belongs here when it has appeared in multiple corrections or clearly governs future decisions.
